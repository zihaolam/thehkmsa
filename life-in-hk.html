Tips for Freshman

Student Visa

After admission to university, university representatives will contact you in regard to the VISA application. You need to follow EVERY step as required by your university. You will need to ship all the required documents to your respective university. If your VISA application has been approved, you will receive a parcel that contains the VISA and related documents.

Before arriving in Hong Kong, remember to paste the VISA on your passport according to the instruction stated on the visa package parcel that you’ve received. Show the immigration officer your VISA and letter of acceptance into university . The officer will give you a landing slip and make sure you enter HK as a STUDENT instead of TOURIST.

If you are having a conditional offer while waiting for your actual results, you may not have your VISA ready before going to Hong Kong. In that case, you may need to enter HK as a tourist and have a trip in Macau. Have fun in Macau and come back to HK again by showing your VISA and enter HK as a student.

**Bear in mind that you need to apply the VISA with the offer letter given by the university. If you happen to transfer to another university, you need to reapply the VISA.

Further information can be found on Immigration Department GovHK.

Banking

Documents required for opening a bank account

  • Passport, student visa and landing slip
  • Student ID Card (if any), University Admission Letter
  • Parents’ passport and landing slip (for students aged under 18)
  • Local residential proof (please contact your residential hall/university for details)
  • Proof of residential address (eg. national ID Card/ driving license/ letter issued by high school, government departments, etc, with residential address stated on it)

Items to Bring

  • Umbrella/ raincoat (MUST! HK rains a lot during September)
  • Formal outfit (useful in many occasions especially for business students)
  • Winter wear
  • Personal clothing
  • Laptop (if you own one) & chargers (no converter needed)
    **if you do not have a laptop, you may opt to purchase it after reaching HK with student discounts.
  • Sufficient cash
  • Personal medications/ First aids
  • Anything you need for your studies (e.g. calculator)
  • Anything that reminds you about Malaysia! (e.g. Milo, Malaysia snacks or even Malaysia flag)
